The Connection Between Climate and Health

Climate change poses significant threats to human health, particularly affecting vulnerable populations like the elderly and children. Rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and changing temperature patterns directly impact health outcomes and require adaptive monitoring solutions.

Health Impact Awareness

Heat-Related Illness

Rising temperatures increase the risk of heat exhaustion and heat stroke, especially in elderly populations.

Air Quality

Climate change affects air quality, potentially worsening respiratory conditions in vulnerable groups.

Displacement

Sea level rise may force communities to relocate, disrupting healthcare access and social support systems.

Emergency Preparedness

Extreme weather events require enhanced monitoring and rapid response systems for at-risk populations.
